## 🔍 Business Problem

HR departments often rely on **static reports and fragmented datasets**, limiting visibility into **employee growth, attrition trends, and workforce composition**.

This lack of real time insights leads to:

- Inefficient workforce planning  
- Delayed decision making  
- Poor visibility into retention risks  

👉 This project solves the problem by building an **interactive HR Analytics Dashboard in Excel**, enabling **data-driven HR decision-making**.

---

## 📁 Dataset Overview

- 👥 **Total Employees:** 100+  

- 🧾 **Key Fields:**
  - Employee Status (Attrition, On-Board, Transfer)  
  - Department, Job Level, Employee Type  
  - Gender, Marital Status  
  - Date of Joining, Experience  

---

## ⚙️ Tools & Techniques

- 📊 Microsoft Excel  
- Pivot Tables & Pivot Charts  
- Slicers for interactivity  
- Data cleaning & transformation  

---

## 📈 Dashboard Features

### 🔑 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Total Employees  
- Active Employees  
- Attrition Count & Attrition Rate (~33%)  
- Transfers  

### 📊 Workforce Analysis
- Year wise employee growth trend  
- Hiring vs Attrition comparison  
- Department-wise distribution  

### 👥 Employee Segmentation
- Gender ratio analysis  
- Job level breakdown (Trainee, Professional, Management, Contract)  
- Employee type (Permanent vs Contract)  

### ⏳ Retention & Stability
- Tenure bands (1+, 3+, 5+ years)  
- Workforce stability metrics  

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- 📉 **Attrition rate (~33%)** highlights potential retention challenges  
- 👶 Workforce is concentrated in early career roles  
- ⚖️ Balanced gender distribution supports diversity tracking  
- 🏢 Operations & Sales dominate workforce  
- 🧠 Presence of long-tenured employees (5+ years) ensures stability  

---

## 🚀 Business Impact

- Enables **data driven HR decisions** with centralized KPI tracking  
- Helps identify **attrition risks and retention gaps**  
- Improves **workforce planning and resource allocation**  
- Replaces manual reporting with **interactive dashboards**
